Step 1: Research Your Options

The first step to studying in the United States is to research your options to find a school that fits your needs. In the united states, there are so many universities that vary in terms of tuition fees, majors, location, and so many other things, therefore, you don’t have to match yourself to the school, but rather find the school that matches your needs and your preferences.

The good thing in the USA is that there is no official ranking system for colleges and universities in the United States. The best college or university is the one that is best for you and meets your requirements—academic, financial, and personal.

However, you need to start your search at least a year and a half prior to the academic year in which you want to attend a US college or university. Here are a few questions you can start by answering to help you in your search:

  • 1) Why do you want to study in the United States?

  • 2) Where will you fit in best?

  • 3) Which colleges or universities will meet your needs?

  • 4) Will you need financial assistance?

  • 5) Where do you want to live in the United States?

  • 6) What are the application and financial aid deadlines?
