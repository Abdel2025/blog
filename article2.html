What to do to study in the U.S.A?

With over 1,000,000 international students seeking to improve their education and life experience, the United States has the world's largest international student population. International students make up over 5% of all students enrolled in higher education in the United States, and the number is growing. International education in the United States has gone a long way since the mid-1950s when international student enrollment was barely around 35,000.

Although the United States is an international-friendly country that welcomes any student from any country in the world, who is seeking to continue his education, there are few things to follow in order to achieve that goal. Generally, to study at a university in the United States, you must first complete four essential steps:

  • 1) College/university search: before getting to decide which university you want to apply for, you will need to know a lot of information to be able to make the good choice. Things such as the tuition, the place where the university is, the living costs …, are very important to know so that you can choose the university that will fit you.

  • 2) Request admission application: after choosing the universities you are interested in; the next step is to contact the Office of Admissions at each school and request an admissions application for graduate study. Each university and college in the United States establishes its own admissions requirements and selects applicants who meet them. As a result, each university or college will require a separate application.

  • 3) Arrange/Submit the documents: After getting the admission application, and knowing what the required documents of each school are, make arrangements to have all of your secondary and post-secondary records sent straight to the schools you've chosen. Then, following the directions in each application packet, send in the applications and application fees.

  • 4) Get accepted & received the I20: after reviewing all your applications and your academic grades, in addition to the test scores (SAT, ACT, TOEFL …), the university will send you the I20 document which you will need in order to apply for the student visa (F-1 visa). Finally, start the procedure to apply for the visa.

A visa will be stamped in your passport when you have been interviewed and approved. Then you're ready to come to the United States and begin your studies. Make your traveling plans ahead of time to avoid being disappointed at the last minute.
