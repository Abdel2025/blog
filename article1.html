Understand education in USA

In Morocco, most universities as well as secondary schools use a 20-point grading scale. Most of the time, formal Moroccan grades are not taken into account in graduate school admittance false assumptions. For example, a grade of 12 (which is rated a passable grade in Morocco but equivalent to only 60% in the United States, where it is considered a below-average) is often a decent starting point for applying for graduate courses and financial aids or scholarships. Indeed, a passing grade is defined as a score of 10 or higher on a scale of 0 to 20, with only a few students averaging more than 14 in all courses.

It would be incorrect to simply multiply a Moroccan grade by a factor of 5 to obtain its equivalent in Canada or the United States. A score of 20 is considered perfect and is almost never given. In general, a 16 is a good grade. A 12 may also be considered a great mark in other cases, particularly when it comes to the Social Sciences or Humanities.

In the United States, the academic grading is generally provided in the form of five, six, or seven letter grades. A+, A, A, B+, B, B, C+, C, C, D+, D, D, and F are the traditional grades, with A+ being the highest and F being the lowest. Grades can also be given numerically in some cases. Numeric-to-letter-grade conversions differ from system to another, as well as between disciplines and status. Your grades do not stand on their own at most universities in the United States. They typically correspond to what is referred to as a quality point. The quality point is a number assigned to your grade, which can then be applied to your GPA.

What is the GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Point Average. It is a standard method of evaluating academic achievement in the U.S. Technically, each course is assigned a set amount of "units" or "credits" based on its content. For example, If you take a three credit class and receive an A grade, you receive 3 credits times 4 points (for the A), which is equal to a total of 12 grade points for the course. Let’s say you also get a B in a four-credit class (common in Mathematics, for example), that’s 4 credits multiplied by 3 points for a total of 12 points. So you've earned 24 grade points for the 7 credits in your two classes. The GPA is calculated by dividing the total grade points by the number of credits. (24/7=3.43), so your GPA is 3.43, which is equivalent to a B+ grade.
