What English tests I need to take?

Most of the American universities require that international students pass certain English tests to show their skills in speaking, writing, and understanding the English language. Here are some of the most commonly used tests:

  • 1) The Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL): this is one of the most common accepted by colleges and universities in the USA as a tool to measure English language proficiency. The TOEFL is offered in both an internet-based format (iBT) and a paper-based format (PBT).

  • 2) The International English Language Testing System (IELTS): The IELTS test is intended to evaluate the language skills of students who want to study or work in an English-speaking environment. It is presented in both an academic version and a general training form.

  • 3) The Cambridge English Advanced (CAE) test: This test is planned for people who need to use English for academic or professional purposes. It is accepted by many universities and employers as evidence of a high level of proficiency in English.

  • 4) The Pearson Test of English (PTE): This test is designed to evaluate the English language skills of people who want to study or work in an English-speaking country. It is accepted by many universities and employers as evidence of English language proficiency.
